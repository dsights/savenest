# SaveNest Solar: Email & SMS Templates

**Philosophy:** These templates are designed to feel like a real human is writing them from their personal inbox. They are short, punchy, and end with a clear Call to Action (CTA) or question. **No corporate speak, no heavy graphics.**

---

### 1. The Immediate Lead Response (Send within 5 minutes of form submission)
*Use this if you can't call them immediately, or send it right before you dial so they know who is calling.*

**Subject:** Your Solar Savings Quote - Quick Question
**Body:**
> Hi [Name],
> 
> I saw you just requested a solar savings calculation for your home in [Postcode]. Based on your quarterly bill of $[BillAmount], you have a lot of room for savings.
> 
> To make sure I get you the most accurate rebate info and match you with the right local installer, I just need to check one detail: **is your roof metal (colorbond) or tile?**
> 
> I'll give you a quick buzz from [Your Phone Number] in a few minutes, or you can just reply directly to this email.
> 
> Cheers,
> [Your Name]
> SaveNest Solar

---

### 2. The "No Answer" Follow-up
*Use this if you called them within 5 minutes but they didn't pick up or went to voicemail.*

**Subject:** Tried to reach you - SaveNest Solar
**Body:**
> Hi [Name],
> 
> I just tried calling about your solar quote request. I've run the preliminary numbers and it looks like you qualify for the 2026 state incentives.
> 
> When is a good time for a quick 2-minute chat to finalize your quotes? 
> 
> Best,
> [Your Name]

---

### 3. The Installer Handoff (The "Warm Intro")
*Use this after you've spoken to the lead, qualified them, and are passing them to your installer partner to close the deal.*

**Subject:** Intro: [Installer Name] / [Lead Name] - Solar Installation
**Body:**
> Hi [Lead Name],
> 
> Great chatting with you earlier. As promised, I'm introducing you to [Installer Name] from [Partner Company]. They are a highly-rated, CEC-accredited installer local to your area and specialize in the systems we discussed.
> 
> [Installer Name], please meet [Lead Name]. They have a $[BillAmount] quarterly bill, a [Metal/Tile] roof, and are looking to eliminate their bill with a 6.6kW system taking advantage of the current rebates.
> 
> I'll let you two take it from here to schedule a quick site inspection or finalize the proposal.
> 
> Cheers,
> [Your Name]

---

### 4. The Mid-Process Check-in (3-5 days after handoff)
*Use this to ensure your installer partners are actually doing their job and the lead isn't going cold.*

**Subject:** How did the quote look?
**Body:**
> Hi [Name],
> 
> Just checking in. Did you end up connecting with [Installer Name] and receiving your quote?
> 
> Let me know if you have any questions about the hardware they proposed or how the rebates were applied. My job is to make sure you're getting a fair, transparent deal.
> 
> Thanks,
> [Your Name]

---

### 5. Post-Sale / The Referral Ask (1-2 weeks after installation)
*This is critical for scaling. Referrals are free leads with the highest closing rate.*

**Subject:** Checking in on your new solar system
**Body:**
> Hi [Name],
> 
> I saw your system was recently installed! Just wanted to reach out and make sure everything went smoothly with the crew and that your new inverter app is tracking your generation.
> 
> By the way, we rely heavily on word of mouth. We are currently offering a **$500 cash referral bonus** if you send a friend, family member, or neighbor our way who ends up installing a system. 
> 
> If anyone comes to mind who hates their power bill as much as you did, just reply with their name and number and I'll take care of the rest.
> 
> Enjoy the cheap power!
> 
> Cheers,
> [Your Name]

---

### 6. The Re-engagement (For leads that went dark after 14-30 days)
*A simple, one-line email to try and revive dead leads.*

**Subject:** Still looking at solar?
**Body:**
> Hi [Name],
> 
> Are you still considering solar for your home, or did you end up putting the project on hold?
> 
> Let me know either way.
> 
> Thanks,
> [Your Name]
