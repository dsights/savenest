# Solar Service Company Execution Plan: $1M in 3 Months

**Disclaimer:** Achieving $1,000,000 in revenue within 3 months as a completely new entrant in the highly regulated Australian solar industry requires an aggressive strategy focused on **Lead Generation and Sales Brokerage**. Securing your own CEC (Clean Energy Council) accreditation, electrical licenses, and installation crews takes 3-6 months. To make money *first*, you will operate as a high-ticket solar broker—generating highly qualified leads and selling the jobs to established, accredited installation companies at a significant margin or per-lead fee.

## The Strategy: The Brokerage Model
Instead of installing panels, you build a brand (SaveNest Solar) that educates homeowners, calculates their savings, and closes the sale or sells the pre-qualified lead to installers who need jobs.

### Revenue Math for $1M in 3 Months:
*   **Average Solar System Cost:** $8,000 - $12,000
*   **Broker Margin / Referral Fee:** 15% - 25% per system sold ($1,500 - $2,500) OR $100-$300 per qualified lead.
*   **To hit $1,000,000 as a broker:** You need to broker approximately **400 - 650 closed solar sales** in 90 days.
*   **Monthly Target:** ~150 - 200 sales per month.
*   **Daily Target:** ~5 - 7 sales per day.

## Month 1: Foundation, Partnerships, and Aggressive Setup
**Goal:** Digital presence live, partnerships signed, marketing funnel ready.

**Week 1: Legal & Branding (The Essentials)**
*   **Action 1:** Register Business Name & ABN/ACN (e.g., SaveNest Energy).
*   **Action 2:** Domain registration and initial landing page deployment (done in this setup).
*   **Action 3:** Setup CRM (HubSpot, GoHighLevel, or Zoho) to manage leads automatically.
*   **Action 4:** Create standard terms & conditions for lead-sharing agreements with installers.

**Week 2: Securing Fulfillment Partners**
*   **Action 1:** Cold call/email Top Tier CEC Accredited Installers in target states (NSW, QLD, VIC have high solar uptake).
*   **Action 2:** Pitch: *"We are a marketing and sales engine launching with a massive ad budget. We don't install. We want to feed you ready-to-sign contracts or highly qualified leads. Can you handle 50 extra installs a month?"*
*   **Action 3:** Sign Service Level Agreements (SLAs) with 3-5 reliable installation partners defining your commission/referral fee.

**Week 3: The Marketing Funnel & Tech Build**
*   **Action 1:** Finalize the "SaveNest Solar Savings Calculator" on the landing page (Lead capture magnet).
*   **Action 2:** Setup Meta (Facebook/Instagram) and Google Ads accounts.
*   **Action 3:** Create initial ad creatives: "Is your Australian home eligible for the solar rebate in 2026? Find out in 30 seconds."
*   **Action 4:** Setup email automation (Drip campaigns for leads who don't convert immediately).

**Week 4: Launch & Test (The Ignition)**
*   **Action 1:** Launch Google Search Ads (High Intent: "solar quotes melbourne", "best solar panels sydney").
*   **Action 2:** Launch Facebook Ads (Broad Audience, Educational angle).
*   **Action 3:** Spend $1,000 - $3,000 this week aggressively testing which ad copy converts cheapest.
*   **Action 4:** Manually call every single lead within 5 minutes of form submission. Qualify them and pass to partners.

---

## Month 2: Optimization and Sales Scaling
**Goal:** Consistent daily lead flow, hiring closers, hitting 5 sales/day.

**Week 5-6: Dialing in the CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**
*   **Action 1:** Analyze Ad data. Kill losing ads, double budget on winning ads.
*   **Action 2:** SEO push. Publish 10 high-quality solar guides on the SaveNest blog (e.g., "Solar Rebates NSW 2026 Explained").
*   **Action 3:** Hire a virtual assistant or local junior sales rep to handle the immediate "5-minute callback" to qualify leads before sending them to installers.

**Week 7-8: High-Ticket Closing**
*   **Action 1:** If your margin is better closing the deal yourself and sub-contracting the install, start doing in-home or zoom consultations.
*   **Action 2:** Scale Ad spend proportionately. If you spend $1 to make $3, spend $10,000/day if cash flow permits.
*   **Action 3:** Introduce SMS marketing for abandoned calculator submissions.

---

## Month 3: The $1M Sprint (Volume & Efficiency)
**Goal:** Maximum ad spend, peak volume, operational efficiency.

**Week 9-10: Nationwide Expansion**
*   **Action 1:** Expand ad targeting to SA, WA, and TAS if partner installers are available.
*   **Action 2:** Introduce a "Refer a Friend" program for your existing Month 1 & 2 customers (e.g., $500 cash back for successful referrals).
*   **Action 3:** Cross-sell existing SaveNest users (finance/insurance) on Solar.

**Week 11-12: The Final Push**
*   **Action 1:** Aggressive retargeting campaigns (Ads showing to people who visited the site but didn't submit).
*   **Action 2:** Optimize the lead handoff process to ensure installers are closing at >20% rate.
*   **Action 3:** Review financials and hit the $1M gross revenue milestone through volume.

---

## Long-Term: Becoming an End-to-End Retailer (Months 4-12)
Once you have cash flow from the brokerage model, you build the actual company infrastructure:
1.  **Month 4:** Apply for Clean Energy Council (CEC) Approved Solar Retailer status.
2.  **Month 5:** Hire an in-house CEC accredited electrical engineer for system design.
3.  **Month 6:** Begin purchasing stock wholesale (panels, inverters, batteries) to increase margins.
4.  **Month 7+:** Transition from sub-contracting to hiring your own installation crews for maximum profit margin and quality control.
